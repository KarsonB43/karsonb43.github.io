Chemistry

Chem 105

Chem 105 is one of BYU's general chemistry classes. It provides an introductory understanding of the elements, what they are composed of, electronegativity, bonding (introductory quantum properties of particles), introductory thermodynamics, acid-base chemistry, and more. This class requires a solid understanding of math including college algebra. You will be using various formulas provided to you on a formula sheet. It is important to know how to manipulate formulas to find desired variables. This class is very damanding and is often referred to as a "weed out course". Tests are challenging and there is a lot of homework required. By keeping up with course materials and using all of the resources available to you, you will be successful in this course.

Estimated Weekly Time Commitment (Hrs/Week):

25

Credit Hours: 4.0

Prerequisites: Math 110 (College Algebra)
