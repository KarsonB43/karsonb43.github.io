Chemistry

Chem 106

Chem 106 is the second semester of BYU's general chemistry classes. It builds upon Chem 105 and explores the elements, what they are composed of, electronegativity, bonding (introductory quantum properties of particles), acid-base chemistry, and other chemical phenomena in greater detail. This class also uses a formula sheet but is generally said to involve less math than Chem 105. The amount of math required may vary by professor. This class is very damanding. By keeping up with course materials and using all of the resources available to you, you will be successful in this course.

Estimated Weekly Time Commitment (Hrs/Week):

20

Credit Hours: 3.0

Prerequisites: Chem 105 (General College Chemistry I)
